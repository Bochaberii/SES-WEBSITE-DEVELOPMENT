WHO WE ARE

The Society of Engineering Students is the official JKUAT Engineering Students Club.Established in 1994, the club has a membership of over 500 students from over 10 engineering disciplines.The club consists of 7 committees namely:Mentorship, editorial, projects, corporates,publicity, outreach and women in technology. Our members belong to various committees based on their interests promoting a holistic growth beyond class work. Through active participation in these committees, students acquire both technical and soft skills, mentor learn from their peers, contribute by motivating younger aspiring engineers.

SES COMMITEES AND CHAIRS

Women in Tech

Victoria Rotich

TIE 3.2

It is abbreviated as WIT, and it is open to all genders. This committee is geared towards ensuring that the percentage of women pursuing careers in STEM continues to grow.

    The roles of WIT include:

  • Organizing events and webinars for women empowerment.
  • Organizing CSR activities to give back to the community.
  • Providing a networking platform among the members and professionals in the field.

Editorial

Sandra Keya

Electrical and electronics engineering, 2.2

Writing is a beautiful art form that aims to inspire, educate, challenge and enlighten readers. As the Editorial Committee we provide a platform for all who aspire to sharpen their skills in writing ,designing and editing to explore those areas. Articles are published weekly on the SES blog .The committee also works on a semester wise newsletter and a magazine at the end of the year. These various activities enhance the skills of students in editing ,writing and designing and provides them with an opportunity to be creative.

Publicity

Cephas Mutua

Electrical and electronics engineering, 2.2

A.K.A ‘ The face of the Society’. We are responsible for all the SES social media handles (Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn) for publicizing the activities of the society. We do this by making posters. Some of the activities we post include upcoming meetings, events and Monday Affirmations. In this committee, one gets to learn how to create and share content and portray messages in graphical forms. We also seek to keep the members engaged in activities such as quiz nights.

Corporates

Simon Gitonga

Electrical and electronics engineering, 2.2

Indeed, there is more to an engineer than just book knowledge. Soft skills are essential if one wants to be competitive and holistic in the job market.. One can simply not rely on theoretical knowledge alone to be marketable in the jobs space. If you are that person who is curious enough to discover what lies beyond what you learn in class, then the Corporates committee is for you. It aims to inculcate the student with skills such as entepreneurial skills, communication skills among others.

Mentorship

  2. Support- Creating a platform where individuals enhance their leadership skills.
  3. Guide and coach- Organize meet-ups and seminars for our members and invite industry professionals.

Projects

Stephen Oduor

Mechanical Engineering 3.2

This committee aims to build a community that collaborates. This inherently fosters creativity and problems will be solved in a much more effective way. If you want to see something that has been learnt in class being applied in real life, consider joining this committee. Meetings are usually held every Thursday from 7pm and various projects are normally demonstrated.

Outreach

Mwanga

Mechatronics 2.2

As the Outreach committee, its all about giving back to the community. This is done by organizing outreach and inreach events. For the inreach, we invite other people to JKUAT (mostly high school students) and offer mentorship to them about the various engineering courses. On the other hand, outreach events entail going out to help in one way or the other. This could be donating to children’s homes or supporting those who need our help. Our slogan is, ‘throw smiles like confetti’, because our mission here is to spread joy by giving back. Karibuni Outreach!
